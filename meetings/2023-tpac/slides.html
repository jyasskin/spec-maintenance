Tracking Specification Maintenance

Jeffrey Yasskin

★ W3C TPAC 2023, Seville, 11–15 September ★

The problem

  • Editors change roles, and stop spending time on old specs.
  • Sometimes that's ok, if the feature is stable.
  • If important issues come up, we don't always notice.
  • Even if we notice, organizations have to reallocate people to respond.

The problem in numbers

  • 411 W3C and WHATWG specs in browser-specs
  • 15924 open issues and PRs
  • Mean issue age: 3 years
  • Mean time since an issue was updated: 2 years

What do we need?

Service Level Objectives

Define when an issue isn't being addressed fast enough.

  • Bugs and enhancements will have different SLOs.
  • Some bugs are low priority and can just stay open.
    • But everything should be triaged.
  • Distinguish bugs endorsed by implementers vs developers?

Reporting

  • Find specs that are behaving worse than average.
  • Automate integration with browser prioritization.
  • Pretty charts motivate management.
  • Help bystanders notice problems.
  • Maybe one will volunteer.
  • Show who to contact.
  • If an editor's not responsive, talk to a team.
